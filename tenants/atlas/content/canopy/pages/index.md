---
title: The Canopy
description: The high green rooms, where the light arrives in pieces and everything has learned to wait.
---

The canopy is the least hurried country we survey. Light does not fall here so much as it is admitted, leaf by leaf, in coins and ribbons that move with the hour; a naturalist who stands still long enough will find the morning delivered to him in instalments. The inhabitants have arranged their whole lives around this rationing. They wait — for the dapple to shift, for the dusk to arrive, for us to look away — and they are, without exception, better at waiting than we are.

We have furnished this wing with what the waiting has so far yielded: a moth that carries its own evening, a mold that will not stop talking, a leaf that is not a leaf, and a weaver whose patience shames the rest of the company, ourselves included. The reader is advised to take the plates on trust and the field notes with sympathy. Everything up here holds still on purpose, and much of what we record was seen once, briefly, and then declined to be seen again.
