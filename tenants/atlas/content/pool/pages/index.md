---
title: The Pool
description: The still water at the glass, where the light bends and the slow things live.
---

Of the three wings of this guide, the pool asks the most patience and repays it most quietly. It is the cool corner of the world under glass — a standing water that does not so much reflect the light as detain it, bending every lamp and every observer into gentle strangers. Nothing here hurries. We have learned to arrive early, sit low, and let the water forget us before we begin to count.

What lives here lives slowly, and mostly at the edges of the day. A shell crosses the pane at the pace of an honest argument; a small gold light burns at middle depth as though it had been dropped there and declined to go out. Some inhabitants we know well enough to greet. One we know only by its voice, and the voice is not its own. The reader is asked to take our records in the spirit they were made: cold hands, low light, and every confidence honestly earned.
