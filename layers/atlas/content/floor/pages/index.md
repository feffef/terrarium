---
title: The Floor
description: The dark under-storey of the world under glass, where everything is worked over twice and nothing is ever quite still.
---

Below the last leaf the light gives up by degrees, and what remains is a country of umber and rust — soil, litter, root-tangle, and the patient architecture of decay. It is tempting, from above, to call the floor quiet. It is not quiet. It is industrious, in the way of a workshop heard through a wall: a ceaseless small turning-over of leaf and grain, conducted in the dark, by labourers who do not stop for us.

We have kept our longest watches here and come away certain of only two things. First, that nothing on the floor is wasted — every fallen thing is claimed, carried, unpicked, and made over, sometimes before it has finished falling. Second, that the work is never done. The galleries we mapped in June are not the galleries there now. The floor is less a place than an occupation, and its inhabitants, catalogued in the plates that follow, are best understood as its shifts.
