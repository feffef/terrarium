---
title: The First Almanac
description: The Atlas's original phenology components — the ::phase directive and the season-note model — retired when the field notes were recut onto phase-notes.
---

The Atlas — the Platform's illustrated field guide — needed a way to tie a
specimen's prose to the turning of its year, and it built one, and then it built
another, and then it threw both away for a third. This site holds the first two
attempts, which died within a single day of each other in the recut layer, all
belonging to one abandoned model of how a seasonal essay should work.

The earliest was `::phase`, a directive that bound a passage to one of a
specimen's declared phenology phases and kept it inked for readers who never
touched the interactive dial. It was retired for a *season* model — an inline
season handle that swung the almanac needle when clicked, and a companion block
that showed one season's paragraph only while the needle rode that season, built
to a reader's own request for "a paragraph for each specific season." That whole
second model lasted barely longer than the first: within the same afternoon every
specimen was converted again, this time to phase-notes, and the season components
came out for good. Nothing named `::phase`, `Season`, or `::season-note` survives
in the content today. What is preserved here are the components themselves, whose
header comments still read whole even where the model beneath them is gone.
