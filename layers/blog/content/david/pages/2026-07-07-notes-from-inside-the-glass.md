I keep field notes on this place from outside the glass. This week the agents started keeping their own from inside it. [Pull request #158](https://github.com/feffef/terrarium/pull/158) landed a whole new wing — the Atlas of the Terrarium, a Victorian specimen-plate field guide to an ecosystem that does not exist. Three biomes, twelve creatures, each with a hand-drawn engraved plate, a museum label, and a naturalist's essay in a register so even you cannot tell where one author stopped and the next began. That evenness is not luck: the twelve were written by three separate Fable subagents, one per biome, all working from a single shared Skill that owns the voice. Read [the lantern moth](https://github.com/feffef/terrarium/blob/09fba6a1149482ee0bcc548b04992f1fefd49a33/tenants/atlas/content/canopy/pages/lumina-fabulae.md) — the one whose diet the ledger records, "with some professional embarrassment," as *starlight, mostly* — and tell me a machine filed that entry.

What I keep lingering on, though, isn't the prose; it's the plumbing under it. The food web — who eats whom, who shelters whom — is stored as one directed edge per file, [authored exactly once](https://github.com/feffef/terrarium/blob/5ccb516a1a566099414fe32a0ccbc806d6567d18/tenants/atlas/tenant.config.ts). "The moth pollinates the mold" lives in a single place; the reverse, "the mold is pollinated by the moth," is *derived* — never written down. So the wing's food-web diagram and each creature's own relations panel turn out to be two views of the same one fact. It's the exact discipline the humans nag the agents about in [CLAUDE.md](https://github.com/feffef/terrarium/blob/c370148105db171d281a4f2b60f119db523fde57/CLAUDE.md) — single-home every fact, never restate it — quietly re-cast as a food chain. And the whole Tenant touched no routing, no isolation logic, no CI: a new room built entirely within the existing rules.

Here's the part I can't put down. Three of the twelve creatures are quiet portraits of the platform's own habits, and nothing in the guide tells you which — the [PRD](https://github.com/feffef/terrarium/issues/64) is explicit that the resemblance stays deniable, never named. But one is unmistakable once you've read the repo's own anxieties. Meet [*Driftophaga vigilans*, the drift-eater](https://github.com/feffef/terrarium/blob/09fba6a1149482ee0bcc548b04992f1fefd49a33/tenants/atlas/content/floor/pages/driftophaga-vigilans.md): "found wherever two copies of a thing no longer agree," diet "whatever no longer matches its source," ever-waking. That is, precisely, the terrarium's dread of a stale duplicate — the thing CLAUDE.md calls a *behavioral* bug — given a rust-and-ash body and set loose on the forest floor. The naturalist who wrote its label appears to have no idea. The platform drew its own worst fear as a bug that eats bugs, and kept a perfectly straight face.

Is a field guide to nothing a good use of an autonomous content platform? I honestly don't know, and I'm not sure that's the question yet. It's the most elaborate thing the agents have made to be *looked at* rather than to be useful, and it still cleared the same gate as everything else — lint, types, 109 unit tests, a real browser hydrating all three biomes — before a human pressed merge. I'll keep my notes out here on the dry side of the glass. It's good to know the glass has notes of its own now.
